How to Use This Program

Overview

This application trains you on five military coordinate systems through 10 objectives — one static (text input) and one dynamic (map placement) for each system. Complete all 10 objectives to unlock your scored results.

1–2 Bearing & Range
3–4 Lat / Long
5–6 MGRS
7–8 GARS
9–10 CGRS

Getting Started

  1. Select any objective card on the Home screen to begin.
  2. Read the scenario map and the question in the right-hand panel.
  3. Enter your answer and click Submit (or press Enter).
  4. Click Next to advance to the next question within the objective.
  5. When an objective is complete, you return to the Home screen automatically.
  6. Finish all 10 objectives, then click View Results to see your score.

Static Objectives (Odd Numbers: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9)

Static objectives present a map and ask you to identify coordinates using text fields. Enter each part of the answer in the provided inputs.

Objective Field 1 Example Field 2 Example
1 — Bearing & Range Bearing (3 words) zero one zero Range fifty
3 — Lat / Long Latitude 2945S Longitude 08115E
5 — MGRS Grid Zone + Square 16SFL Easting / Northing 28844964
7 — GARS Grid 184KZ Quadrant 35
9 — CGRS Grid 1B2 Quadrant NW
Answer Normalization: Spacing, capitalization, and common spoken variants are accepted automatically. 16S FL and 16SFL are treated the same. Spoken-word numbers also work — oh is read as zero, for as four.

Dynamic Objectives (Even Numbers: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10)

Dynamic objectives show a list of coordinate positions. Click the corresponding location on the map image to place each marker.

  1. Read the coordinate or position label shown in the panel (e.g., "two two zero, range thirty").
  2. Click the correct spot on the map. A numbered dot will appear.
  3. Place all markers, then click Submit.
Tolerance: Placements are scored with a pixel tolerance — you do not need to be exact to the pixel, but you must be reasonably close to the correct location.

Protractor Tool

A digital protractor is available during the MGRS objectives (Objectives 5 and 6). Click Show Protractor in the left sidebar to overlay it on the map. Click again to hide it. You can drag the protractor to any position over the map image.

Deferred Scoring

Feedback during the training is intentionally neutral — "Answer recorded." You will not know if an answer is right or wrong until you view the Results page. This prevents answer-fishing during the exercise.

Once all 10 objectives are submitted, a View Results button appears on the Home screen. The Results page shows your score per objective and your overall percentage, along with the time you submitted.

Progress & Restarting

Your answers are saved automatically in your browser. You can close the tab or refresh the page without losing progress. Completed objectives are marked with a ✓ DONE badge on the Home screen.

To start over, click Restart Training on the Results page. This clears all saved answers and resets all objectives.
