This guide provides a comprehensive overview of identifying, determining, and converting Military Grid Reference System (MGRS) coordinates with a specific focus on 10-meter (8-digit) precision.MGRS Navigation & Coordinate Identification Guide1. Identifying 10-Meter MGRS CoordinatesTo identify an 8-digit coordinate on a military map, follow the cardinal rule: "Read Right, then Up."Step-by-Step Identification:Locate the GZD and 100k Square: Find the large grid identification in the map legend or grid boundaries.Read the Easting (Right): _ Find the vertical grid line to the left of the point.The first two digits of your Easting (e.g., 23) are the large numbers printed on the map's margin for that line.The next two digits (e.g., 45) represent the tens and units of meters moving right from that line.Read the Northing (Up): _ Find the horizontal grid line below the point.The first two digits of your Northing (e.g., 67) are the large numbers printed on the margin.The next two digits (e.g., 89) represent the tens and units of meters moving up from that line.3. Determining MGRS for Marked PositionsWhen you have a point marked on a physical map, you must use a GTA 05-02-012 Coordinate Scale and Protractor (or similar) to extract the 10-meter data.Using the Protractor:Select the Correct Scale: Ensure the protractor scale matches your map scale (e.g., 1:24,000 or 1:50,000).Align the Easting:Place the vertical scale of the protractor on the vertical grid line to the left of your point.Slide the protractor right until the horizontal scale's zero-point is on that grid line and the vertical scale intersects your marked point.Read the value where the point touches the scale. For 10m precision, you are looking for two digits (e.g., 45).Align the Northing:While keeping the protractor horizontally aligned, look at the vertical scale markings.Read the value where the point sits on the vertical scale to get your two Northing digits (e.g., 89).Combine: Combine the grid line numbers with your measured digits: 23 + 45 and 67 + 89 = 2345 6789.4. Converting MGRS CoordinatesConversion typically involves moving between precision levels or translating MGRS to/from Latitude and Longitude.Precision Conversion (Rounding/Truncating)To convert a 1-meter (10-digit) coordinate to a 10-meter (8-digit) coordinate, truncate the last digit of both the Easting and Northing. Do not round up.From: 18S UJ 23456 67891 (1-meter)To: 18S UJ 2345 6789 (10-meter)Digital Conversion LogicWhen developing software tools or using digital systems, conversion follows the UTM (Universal Transverse Mercator) projection.MGRS to Lat/Long: \* Identify the UTM Zone and Hemisphere from the GZD.Calculate the full UTM Easting and Northing using the 100k Square ID offsets.Apply the inverse Transverse Mercator projection to find the geodetic coordinates.Lat/Long to MGRS:Convert decimal degrees to UTM coordinates.Identify the 100k square based on the UTM Easting/Northing.Reduce the UTM Easting/Northing to the remainder of 100,000.Truncate to the desired number of digits (8 digits for 10m).5.
